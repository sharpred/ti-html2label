Curriculum Information

Maths – what parents need to know:

  • Nine year olds now need to know all their tables up to 12x12.
  • Maths facts should be learned by heart. Calculators should not be used until mental and written methods have been mastered at the end of primary school.
  • Basic fractions such as ½ and ¼ are now introduced at age four five.
  • By the end of Year 1, children will be expected to count up to 100. Five year olds will also need to learn number bonds to 20.
  • By the end of primary school, children should add and subtract fractions – this was previously covered at secondary school.

English – what parents need to know:

  1. Commas and apostrophes will be taught to six year olds.
  2. As well as punctuation, there is an emphasis on vocabulary, grammar and spelling at all ages.
  3. Children should participate in discussions from the age of five.
  4. From age five onwards, children will listen and react to poetry at a level higher than their reading level.
  5. The new curriculum includes spelling lists for ages 7 – 9 and 9 – 11.
  6. This is an example link: www.example.com

How can you help

  • Practise adding pairs of numbers by starting with the biggest number.
  • Investigate fractions through real-life situations such as sharing chocolate, cake or pizza.
  • Use a hundred square to support your child with number and place value.
  • Ask your child to turn your shopping list into a sentence using commas to separate the items.
  • Play grammar games such as ‘odd one out’, where your child has to find one noun out of three words.
  • Talk to your child as much as possible about things they watch and experience. Ask their opinion.
  • Share poetry as well as books with your child. Have fun performing poems together.
  • Use spelling cards to practise tricky words.

Absence / Attendance

The following is an extract from our Attendance Policy to clarify the responsibility of parents and carers. Raising attendance is a key school priority and we will continue to monitor attendance on a weekly basis. We were delighted with the improvements we were able to make last year and can only hope to continue with this trend into the future. Parents/carers are asked to:

  1. Wrap any authorised days of term time absence (for exceptional circumstances) around existing school holiday periods to minimise time out of school;
  2. Weigh the benefits to the family of term time absences against detriment to the child’s/children’s educational progress and attainment;
  3. Consider whether it is wholly necessary for the child/children to accompany adults in some circumstances;
  4. Avoid requesting term time absences during periods where there are National Curriculum assessments (see Term Dates for information about these);
  5. Attempt to ensure their child attends school regularly in accordance with the signed Home School Partnership agreement;
  6. Notify the school office by phone/letter by 9am on the first day of any unforeseen absence (e.g. sickness/ family emergency), and everyday thereafter.
  7. The full attendance policy is available on our website: www.example.com.

Dogs and Puppies

There are approximately ten million dogs kept in the UK [Pet Food Manufacturers' Association (PFMA) 2014].

There are many breeds and types of dog, all varying in size, body shape and personality.

On average, dogs live for around twelve years, but many live for much longer.

Your duty to care

Owning and caring for a dog is great fun and immensely rewarding. But it is also a big responsibility. If you own or are responsible for a dog, even on a temporary basis, you are required under the Animal Welfare Act to care for him/her properly.

Understanding dogs' needs

Dogs have very complex needs so looking after them well can be challenging.

There is no one 'perfect' way to care for all dogs because every dog and every situation is different. It is up to you how you look after your dog, but you must take reasonable steps to ensure that you meet all your dog's needs.

Read our expert reviewed pet care information to find out more about the needs of dogs: Environment, Diet, Behaviour, Company, Health and welfare.
